Proven 

hair loss treatments

There are only 3 scientifically proven ways to slow down or reverse male balding. Most treatments of hair loss are through medicines that go on the scalp or that come in pills. Sometimes surgery is used to treat people with areas of hair that cannot grow back.

LEARN MORE

Finasteride (Propecia)

By the far the most effective treatment we have today - Propecia is a drug that directly stops your body producing DHT (Dihydrotestosterone) - the stuff that is responsible for your hair loss.

By directly reducing the amount of this in your body - propecia gets to the root (no pun intended) of the problem and can really slow down and even reverse hair loss.

This medicine is meant for men.

It comes in a pill, and you need a prescription to buy it.

Men can take finasteride by mouth at a dose of 1 milligram (mg) per day. 

Minoxidil - (Rogaine)

Minoxidil differs from propecia in that it doesn’t directly address the cause of hair loss. Instead, minoxidil is believed to widen the blood vessels that reach the hair follicle (vasodilation) - allowing more vital nutrients to reach the hair, and thus slowing down the problem.

While many people experience great results with Minoxidil - because it doesn’t directly target the cause of hair loss - its results are limited when compared with Finasteride.

This medicine is used in men and women.

You can buy it without a prescription. It comes in a liquid or foam that you put on your head.

How to apply

Minoxidil is a scalp treatment, not a hair treatment. One milliliter of the liquid or foam should be applied to the affected areas of the scalp using a dropper or pump spray device. The solution should be lightly spread over the affected area with a finger. People using minoxidil must have a normal, healthy scalp since cuts or openings can allow the solution to be absorbed into the bloodstream.

What can I expect?

Minoxidil must be used for at least six months to determine if it is effective. When it is effective, you usually begin to shed less hair within two months after the start of treatment, and by four to eight months hair begins to grow. The effects of minoxidil usually stabilize after 12 to 18 months of use.

Treatment with minoxidil must be continued indefinitely. If you stop using it, any hair that you've kept or regrown as a result of the medication will be lost.

Not everyone benefits from minoxidil. The best results are seen by those who have had baldness for less than five years, when it affects the crown (top) of the head, and when the area of hair loss is less than 10 centimeters in diameter. 


Ketocanazole (Nizoral)

A shampoo that has been shown to have moderate effects on hair loss - it works by ensuring the scalp is healthy and free of sebum (an oily substance that builds up on the scalp and can damage hair growth).

This medicine is used in men and women.

You can buy it without a prescription. It comes in a liquid or foam that you put on your head.

These three are the only drugs that are scientifically proven to safely slow down or reverse your hair loss.
