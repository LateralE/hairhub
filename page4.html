What causes Hair Loss?

First things first - there are actually many different kinds of hair loss. 

The most common, and the one we will focus on is - male pattern baldness - what is scientifically known as androgenetic alopecia.

This is the kind of hair loss that many men will experience - in fact - 30% of men will start going bald by the age of 30, and up to 50% of men by the age of 50 - so you’re not alone!

Male pattern baldness is caused by a build up something called DHT - DiHydroTestosterone.

What is DHT and why does it cause hair loss?


DHT is an androgen - that is - a hormone - which is a product of testosterone. In short - it is one of the many hormones responsible for making you a man.

Without getting too bogged down in the scientific nitty gritty - the reason DHT causes hair loss is actually a bit complicated.

The basic reason that DHT is believed to cause hair loss is that DHT attaches to the receptors in the hair follicles (‘Dermal Papillas’) - and prevents the necessary nourishment of the hair - a process called miniturization - where the hairs gradually get smaller over time.

However, having a lot of DHT in your system - doesn’t necessarily mean you’ll go bald. A lot of scientists believe that genetic factors play a more crucial role - meaning you can have high or low levels of DHT and still go bald or keep your hair into your 80s.

But...?

My dad isn’t bald - so why am I going bald?

Unfortunately, the exact factors that determine whether a person goes bald are still a bit of a mystery.

While genetic factors clearly play a significant role, the exact genetic makeup of every person is different - meaning it can be hard to predict whether you’ll go bald.

I have thick hair, does that mean I won’t go bald?

This is a persistent and completely untrue myth. There is no evidence at all that the thickness, quality or colour of your hair has any effect on male pattern baldness.

How does hair loss progress?

Male pattern baldness is classified according to what’s known as the Norwood Scale.

While every person’s hair loss and head shape is different - we can’t confidently say that someone is balding until they go past stage 2 in this scale. 


The further along this scale you are the more hair grafts - groups of hair - you will need for a hair transplant.

Is there a cure?

Unfortunately, for now - there isn’t a cure available for hair loss.

However, there are now several treatments and cosmetic procedures that can really help you - there’s never been a better time to start balding!

The main treatment that works is called Finasteride (brand name Propecia) - a drug that directly targets the cause of hair loss.

Minoxidil (brand name Rogaine/Regaine) and Ketocanazole shampoo (Nizoral) are also useful additions that can slow down the progression of male pattern baldness.
