What to expect from a hair transplant

Undergoing a hair transplant is an important decision which you should consider carefully and get informed about.

1) You won't look like you did when you were 16

Firstly - it is important to understand that hair transplantation - unless you’ve got very little or early hair loss - cannot make your hair look like it did when you were 16. This is simply because hair transplantation involves taking hair from areas of your head that won’t go bald - the back and sides - and using it to cover areas you want hair.

As a result - it will never quite as thick as your original hair. Any surgeon or company that says that they can make your hair look identical to your original hair is promising too much...

But you shouldn’t give up just yet!

Hair transplants performed by skilled surgeons can still achieve beautiful results - and a drastic improvement in your appearance. The goal of a hair transplant is to achieve the illusion of coverage - using the transplanted hair to create a natural-looking, aesthetic new head of hair.

The good news in this regard is that hair transplant techniques have improved massively in the last 15 years - and the number of high-quality surgeons has increased hugely. 

2) Your hair won’t grow in overnight.

If you talk to any hair transplant veteran - they will tell you that the main thing to bear in mind when undergoing a hair transplant is patience.

Understandably, you may be anxious to see your great new head of hair soon after the procedure. Unfortunately, hair takes time to take root and grow. 

Most surgeons advise that the first significant results will start to appear between 3-4 months post op, with 80% of the result coming in at 6 months, and the full result usually coming in at 12 months after the procedure.
